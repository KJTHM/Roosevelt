# Roosevelt Academy — Voice Guide

A working reference for writing and auditing copy. Goal: read like a real, established
school — not a nonprofit fundraising pitch and not AI filler. Write to this guide; don't fight it.

---

## 1. Three voice adjectives

**Confident · Plain · Warm**

- **Confident** — state what the school does, in the present tense. "Roosevelt serves grades 8–12."
  Not "aims to" / "strives to" / "is designed to."
- **Plain** — short Anglo-Saxon words, concrete nouns, one idea per sentence. A parent skims this on a phone.
- **Warm** — warmth comes from specifics and respect (small classes, a real person calls you back),
  not from adjectives about feelings. Show the care; don't announce it.

> One-line case: a parent choosing a school for a vulnerable kid is buying *trust*. Trust comes from
> a school that sounds like it already knows what it does — calm and specific — not one straining to
> reassure. Confident + plain + warm is how established schools earn that on their own sites.

---

## 2. Do / Don't table

| DO (institutional-school voice) | DON'T (fundraising pitch / therapy-speak / AI filler) |
|---|---|
| Lead with what's taught and how. "Reading, writing, math, science, on an individualized plan." | Lead with feelings. "where they're seen, safe, and celebrated." |
| Present tense, active voice. "We design lessons around each student." | Hedge. "Our approach is designed to help students feel supported." |
| One concrete fact a competitor can't copy. "A member of our team follows up within two business days." | Stack abstractions. "individualized, meaningful, and built to last." |
| Name the grade range plainly. "Grades 8–12." | Inflate scope. "ages 2 to 22" (factually wrong — fix on sight). |
| Say it once. | Repeat the comfort line. "safe and understood" 3+ times across a page. |
| Respect the reader's intelligence. "If we're not the right fit, we'll point you to a school that is." | Emotional fundraising bait. "Somewhere, a child is waiting for their classroom." |
| Quote a principle, then move on. "Growth begins when you find the right environment." | "We believe…" opening every other paragraph. |
| Specific outcomes. "transition into post-secondary education, trade programs, or employment." | Empty uplift cluster. "thrive, journey, fullest potential, empower, unlock." |

## 3. Five before → after rewrites (from Roosevelt's own current copy)

All "before" lines are live in `index.html`.

1. **Before:** "Every child deserves a safe harbor to learn." *(empty uplift + therapy-speak; says nothing)*
   **After:** "A day school for students in grades 8–12 with learning differences, in Austin."

2. **Before:** "…in a place where they're seen, safe, and celebrated." *(rule-of-three therapy-speak)*
   **After:** "…taught the way they learn best, in small classes with on-site therapy."

3. **Before:** "Roosevelt Academy is a nonprofit day school for children ages 2 to 22…" *(factual error)*
   **After:** "Roosevelt Academy is a nonprofit day school for students in grades 8–12…"
   *(Hard fact: Roosevelt serves grades 8–12. "Ages 2–22" is wrong template text — fix every instance,
   including the `programs.html` meta description that still reads "ages 2–22.")*

4. **Before:** "You don't have to have it all figured out. You just have to find the right place
   for your child to learn. You don't have to find it alone." *(anaphora + hollow reassurance)*
   **After:** "Not sure if Roosevelt is the right fit? Reach out anyway. If we're not, we'll point you
   to a school that is." *(this line already exists on programs.html — it's the good version)*

5. **Before:** "Too many families are told their child is 'too much' for a classroom, or handed a
   waitlist instead of a welcome." *(fundraising-pitch emotion)*
   **After:** "We built Roosevelt for students who learn differently and need a school built around
   how they learn." *(state who it's for; drop the grievance framing)*

## 4. The AI-tell checklist (detect it, never write it)

- **Rule-of-three abstraction padding** — "individualized, meaningful, and built to last."
- **"Not just X, but Y"** / "It's not about A, it's about B."
- **Empty uplift clusters** — *meaningful, fulfilling, thrive, journey, fullest potential, unlock,
  empower, foster, deserve, holistic, passionate.*
- **"We believe…" anaphora** stacked across paragraphs.
- **Hollow reassurance repeated** — the same "safe / private / understood" comfort line 3×.
- **Hedging** — "designed to help / aims to / strives to" instead of "does."
- **Mirror-the-prompt openers** — "In today's world, education matters more than ever."
- **Therapy-speak (school-specific)** — "seen, safe, and celebrated," "students feel understood."
- **Fundraising-pitch emotion (school-specific)** — "a child is waiting for their classroom."
- **Intensifying language** — "actually learn," "real academics," "truly known," "genuinely care."
  These imply everything else is fake. Just state the thing: "learn," "academics," "known."

**School-specific tells observed in Roosevelt's own copy (add to watch list):**
- **"deserve" as a load-bearing verb** — appears 6+ times across who-we-are.html. One use is a stance;
  six is a tic. Cut to at most one per page.
- **"reach their fullest potential" / "thrive"** — recurring close. Replace with a concrete outcome.
- **Em-dash dramatic reveal** — "We also have to admit there was a second reason…" Reads as AI pacing.
- **"journey" for schooling** — "educational journey." Just say "their time at Roosevelt" or "school."

## 5. Note on quoted exemplars

Emulated, established schools that describe themselves with confidence and specifics on their own sites:
- **Gow School** (learning differences) — opens with direct address and a hard credential, then a
  concrete metric: "a premier boarding and day school for students with Learning Differences in grades 5
  through Post Graduate," "a low 4:1 student-faculty ratio." Lead with the fact, let the metric carry warmth.
- **Academy of St. Louis** (special needs) — plain subject-verb opener: "The Academy of St. Louis
  assists K-12th-grade students with cognitive disabilities," and names real pathways ("post-secondary
  education, trade programs"). Specific grade range + specific outcomes.
- **The Lawrenceville School** (traditional independent) — present-tense, active, structural facts:
  "Each Lawrenceville student belongs to a House." Values stated once, then concrete.
- **Rice University** (admissions) — one unmistakable, copyable fact: "As a loan-free institution, we
  invest in our students so they can graduate without the burden of student debt."

What they all do that Roosevelt should: open with a plain statement of what the school *is* and *who it
serves*, back warmth with one concrete number or pathway, and say each comforting thing exactly once.
