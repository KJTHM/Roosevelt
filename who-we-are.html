About Us

About Roosevelt Academy

At Roosevelt Academy, we believe that every student deserves an education designed around who they are, not around a standardized system.

Founded by parents and educators in Austin, Texas, with firsthand experience navigating the challenges of special education, Roosevelt Academy was created with one guiding belief: when we tailor education to the individual, students can achieve extraordinary growth. We recognize that every learner has unique strengths, interests, abilities, and goals. Instead of expecting students to fit into a predetermined curriculum, we design learning experiences that meet them where they are and help them move confidently toward what they are capable of achieving.

Our mission extends beyond earning a diploma. We are preparing students for meaningful, independent, and fulfilling lives through individualized academics, communication, executive functioning, social development, life skills, vocational exploration, community engagement, and real-world experiences.

We believe success should be measured by progress, confidence, independence, and purpose, not by comparing one student to another.

Our Vision

Our Vision

We envision a future where students with diverse learning needs have access to an educational community that celebrates their individuality, while equipping them with the tools to thrive as adults.

We are building a school where families feel supported, students feel understood, and every learner is empowered to reach their fullest potential.

Our Phased Approach

Roosevelt Academy is intentionally being built in phases to ensure we create a thoughtful, high-quality program that grows alongside our students.

Phase One

Our inaugural program will serve students in 8th through 12th grade, providing highly individualized education focused on academic growth, executive functioning, communication, life skills, vocational readiness, and preparing students for successful transitions into adulthood. This initial phase allows us to build the personalized culture and programming that are at the heart of Roosevelt Academy.

Looking Ahead

Our vision extends well beyond high school.

Future phases include expanding our programming to support our students into the future. We envision transition, vocational, and adult learning opportunities that continue helping young adults develop independence, meaningful employment, lifelong learning, and strong community connections.

Because education doesn't end with graduation, and neither should support.

At Roosevelt Academy, we're not simply opening a school. We're building a community and a lifelong pathway where every student has the opportunity to learn, grow, contribute, and thrive.

Our Story

Our Name & Mascot

Why Roosevelt?

Our name was inspired by Theodore Roosevelt, not because he lived an easy life, but because he demonstrated what is possible when determination, courage, and purpose come together.

Despite facing significant personal challenges, Roosevelt accomplished more in a remarkably short period of time than many accomplish in a lifetime. He believed in action over excuses, in perseverance over limitations, and in creating opportunities where others saw obstacles.

That spirit became the foundation of our school.

We believe every student deserves to be seen for what they can accomplish and not defined by a diagnosis, a disability, or a standardized expectation. Like Roosevelt, our students have unique strengths waiting to be discovered and developed. We exist to help them build confidence, independence, and meaningful lives by focusing on their abilities instead of their limitations.

Growth begins when you find the right environment.

— The Hermit Crab Principle

Our Mascot

The Hermit Crab Principle

Growth begins when you find the right environment.

Just as a hermit crab periodically searches for a new shell that better fits its growth, every child deserves an educational environment where they feel safe, understood, and challenged in the right ways. At Roosevelt Academy, we believe students flourish when the environment adapts to them, not the other way around.

We also have to admit there was a second reason the hermit crab felt like the perfect mascot...

Many of our students are perfectly content being "hermits" every now and then. They often recharge in quiet spaces, prefer smaller groups, and flourish in environments where they feel safe and understood. We don't see that as something to change. We see it as something to respect.

At Roosevelt Academy, we believe every student deserves a place where they can be themselves, grow at their own pace, and emerge with new skills and confidence when they're ready.

Our Philosophy

Every Student. Their Own Path.

At Roosevelt Academy, we believe every student deserves an education that recognizes who they are, how they learn, and what they need to thrive. Our approach is rooted in connection, flexibility, and the belief that growth looks different for every child.

We know that students are most successful when they feel safe, understood, and supported. That is why we create individualized learning experiences that honor each student's strengths, build confidence, and provide the structure they need to take meaningful steps forward.

Our philosophy is simple: meet students where they are, help them discover what is possible, and walk beside them as they build the academic, social, and life skills needed for their future.

Partnership

Families & Educators: One Team, One Goal

At Roosevelt Academy, we believe the most successful educational journeys are built on partnership.

Parents know their children better than anyone. They understand what motivates them, what challenges them, what brings them joy, and what success looks like beyond the classroom. Our educators bring professional expertise, creativity, and a commitment to individualized learning. Together, we create something neither could accomplish alone.

We don't believe education should happen in isolation. It should be a collaborative process where families, educators, therapists, and support professionals work together with one shared purpose: helping every student reach their fullest potential.

Communication is at the heart of that partnership.

Families can expect regular updates, meaningful conversations, collaborative goal setting, and opportunities to celebrate progress together. We believe parents should always feel connected to their child's educational journey.

As students grow, we also invite them into the conversation. Learning to advocate for themselves, make choices, and participate in setting goals is an essential part of developing confidence and independence.

At Roosevelt Academy, we are more than a school community, we are a team united by one belief: When families and educators work together, students thrive.

Founding families

Seven families. Seven students. One school.

The Roosevelt Academy was founded by seven families and seven incredible students who inspired us to stop waiting for the "right" school and start building it ourselves.

Like so many parents of neurodiverse learners, we had experienced the frustration of trying to fit extraordinary children into systems that weren't designed with them in mind. We knew there had to be another way: one that celebrated strengths, nurtured independence, and prepared students for meaningful, fulfilling lives.

Our founding families didn't want to settle for "good enough." They chose to advocate, to dream bigger, and to create something that didn't yet exist.

While our students may each have different abilities, personalities, and paths, they share one thing in common: they deserve to be known, challenged, encouraged, and believed in.

Today, those seven students are at the heart of everything we do. They remind us why Roosevelt Academy exists and why we'll continue growing.
