Who We Are

Built by families. Designed for every student.

Roosevelt Academy was founded by parents and educators with firsthand experience navigating the challenges of special education. We created this school with one guiding belief: when education is tailored to the individual, students achieve extraordinary growth.

Our story

Why Roosevelt?

Our name was inspired by Theodore Roosevelt — not because he lived an easy life, but because he demonstrated what is possible when determination, courage, and purpose come together. Despite facing significant personal challenges, he believed in action over excuses, in perseverance over limitations, and in creating opportunities where others saw obstacles.

That spirit became the foundation of our school. We believe every student deserves to be seen for what they can accomplish — not defined by a diagnosis, a disability, or a standardized expectation. Our students have unique strengths waiting to be discovered. We exist to help them build confidence, independence, and meaningful lives.

Growth begins when you find the right environment.

— The Hermit Crab Principle

Mission & values

What we stand for

Our mission: to give every child with special needs a real education in a place where they are safe, known, and free to become exactly who they are.

Every student. Their own path.

We design learning experiences that meet students where they are — honoring their strengths, building confidence, and providing the structure they need to take meaningful steps forward.

Families & educators: one team.

Parents know their children better than anyone. Our educators bring professional expertise and creativity. Together, we create something neither could accomplish alone.

Progress, not comparison.

Success is measured by confidence, independence, and purpose — not by comparing one student to another.

Our approach

Built in phases, designed to last.

Phase One

Our inaugural program serves students in 8th through 12th grade, providing highly individualized education focused on academic growth, executive functioning, communication, life skills, vocational readiness, and preparing students for successful transitions into adulthood.

Looking Ahead

Our vision extends well beyond high school. Future phases include transition, vocational, and adult learning opportunities that continue helping young adults develop independence, meaningful employment, and strong community connections.

Because education doesn't end with graduation, and neither should support.

Founding families

Seven families. Seven students. One school.

Roosevelt Academy was founded by seven families and seven incredible students who inspired us to stop waiting for the right school and start building it ourselves. Like so many parents of neurodiverse learners, we had experienced the frustration of trying to fit extraordinary children into systems that weren't designed with them in mind.

Our founding families didn't want to settle for good enough. They chose to advocate, to dream bigger, and to create something that didn't yet exist. Today, those seven students are at the heart of everything we do.

Our mascot

The Hermit Crab.

Just as a hermit crab searches for a new shell that better fits its growth, every child deserves an educational environment where they feel safe, understood, and challenged in the right ways. Many of our students are perfectly content being hermits every now and then — recharging in quiet spaces, preferring smaller groups, and flourishing where they feel understood. We don't see that as something to change. We see it as something to respect.
